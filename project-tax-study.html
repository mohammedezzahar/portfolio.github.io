The Moroccan Free Trade Zone context is a distinctive emerging-market specialization that no European candidate can replicate.

This comprehensive econometric study models the Corporate Tax (IS) impact for enterprises transitioning into designated Free Trade Zones, leveraging Python and Power BI to forecast strategic investment incentives over a multi-year horizon.

The Challenge

Navigating Moroccan tax laws is complex for foreign and domestic institutional investors. Manually calculating the long-term ROI impact of transitioning operations to a Free Trade Zone under the Moroccan Finance Law is prone to error and lacks predictive scenario analysis.

The Solution & Execution

I developed a diagnostic data model utilizing Python (pandas) and EViews to programmatically evaluate multi-variable tax scenarios. By structuring the analysis in interactive Power BI dashboards, I successfully projected a 20% corporate tax (IS) saving over a 5-year investment period, providing the executive board with a clear, data-driven mandate for restructuring.

Methodology

Contextual Analysis

Mapped the latest reforms in the Moroccan Finance Law against institutional investment benchmarks.

Risk Quantification

Modeled potential fiscal liabilities under various regulatory scenarios using advanced Excel simulations.

Strategic Roadmap

Delivered a prioritized list of compliance actions and optimization opportunities for the stakeholder board.

Project Conclusion

The study has since served as a key reference for tax planning within the organization, leading to more informed investment decisions in the Moroccan market.
